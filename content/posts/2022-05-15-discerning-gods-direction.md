In Acts 11, Peter relates his experience with Cornelius to church leaders in Jerusalem. While they initially are critical of Peter's willingness to go into the home of Cornelius, they carefully discern God's working and moving among Gentiles. In the end, they praise God for bringing repentance and true life to those once considered an outsider.
