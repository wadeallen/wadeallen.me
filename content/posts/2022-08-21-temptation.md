The final sentence in the Lord's Prayer is puzzling. James tells us that God does not tempt us, so why would be pray that God would not lead us into temptation. Certainly, the evil one is the tempter who is alive and active in our world today. This portion of the prayer asks God to spare us from the tests and trials of the evil one. As we live out this prayer, we must rely on Jesus to rescue us from the schemes of the evil one. Jesus faced temptation and conquered it. Our attitude toward trials and temptations should be one of humility and dependence on Jesus.
